Ductless Mini-Splits

Zoned comfort, no ductwork needed.

Mini-splits heat and cool specific rooms without tearing up walls for ducts. Ideal for additions, converted attics, historic homes, and any space that runs too hot or too cold.

When mini-splits make sense

Good fits for Toledo-area homes:

  • Room additions or finished attics without duct runs
  • Older homes where adding central air would mean tearing into plaster walls
  • Garages, workshops, or detached structures that need climate control
  • Second floors that run hot in summer and cold in winter
  • Homes with radiator heat that don't have existing AC ductwork
  • Sunrooms, offices, and other rooms with a different comfort profile than the rest of the house

Do mini-splits heat in Ohio winters?

Yes. Modern cold-climate mini-splits (Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat, Trane XV20) are rated to heat efficiently down to -13 degrees. Toledo rarely gets that cold. A properly sized cold-climate unit will keep a space comfortable all winter without backup electric resistance strips in most cases.

That said, for a whole-home primary heating system, we often recommend pairing a mini-split with either a gas backup or a natural-gas furnace for the coldest weeks.

Brands we install

Mitsubishi Electric is our primary mini-split line (best cold-climate performance, 12-year warranty on compressors). Trane makes solid mini-splits as well and integrates well with their conventional HVAC lineup. We install both.

Rebates and tax credits

Cold-climate heat pump mini-splits may qualify for federal energy efficiency tax credits (25C) worth up to $2,000. Ohio utility rebates vary. We can tell you what applies at quote time, but the final paperwork is between you and your utility or tax preparer.

Frequently asked

How much is a mini-split installed in Toledo?

Single-zone installs typically run $3,800 to $6,800 depending on capacity. Multi-zone systems scale from there. Get a site visit for accurate pricing, the variables are mount height, line-set length, and electrical work needed.

Do mini-splits heat in Ohio winters?

Cold-climate models (Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat, Trane XV20) heat efficiently down to -13F. Toledo rarely drops below that. For whole-home primary heat, we often recommend pairing with a gas backup for the coldest weeks.

One zone or multi-zone?

Single-zone is cheaper upfront and more efficient for one room. Multi-zone makes sense if you're conditioning three or more separate spaces. Two-zone is the in-between call that depends on layout.

Are rebates available?

Federal 25C tax credit covers qualifying heat pumps up to $2,000. Ohio utility rebates vary by provider and change yearly. We'll tell you what likely applies during the quote.

How loud are they?

Indoor units are rated 19-30 decibels, quieter than a whisper. Outdoor condensers are 45-55 decibels, about the same as a conversation. Mitsubishi is the quietest. Placement matters, we plan for bedroom walls and nearby windows.
