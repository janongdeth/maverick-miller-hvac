Whole-Home Air Filtration in Toledo, OH

Cleaner air without the marketing gimmicks. Media air cleaners, UV purifiers, and HEPA systems. We tell you what actually helps and what's a waste of money.

When to consider air filtration upgrades

Signs your indoor air could use help:

  • Allergies that get worse indoors
  • Visible dust buildup within a day or two of cleaning
  • Pet dander accumulating on furniture and bedding
  • Smoke smell lingers after cooking or fireplace use
  • Static electricity in winter (low humidity)
  • Windows fogging in winter (high humidity)
  • Newer tight home that feels stuffy
  • Someone in the house has asthma, COPD, or immune issues

What actually works (and what doesn't)

The honest breakdown:

  • MERV 13 filter upgrade: Cheap, effective, 80% of the benefit for 10% of the cost. We recommend this first for most homes.
  • Media air cleaner (4-6" filter): Upgrade from a 1" pleated filter. Captures more, lasts longer (6-12 months instead of 1-3). Good value.
  • UV light in the coil: Real benefit for mold prevention on the evaporator coil. Marginal benefit for airborne viruses or bacteria despite marketing claims.
  • Whole-home HEPA bypass: Significant benefit for people with serious respiratory issues or pet-heavy homes. Expensive but effective.
  • Humidifier (whole-home): Real fix for winter dry air. Cheaper than you'd think.
  • Ionic / ozone air "purifiers": We don't install these. Ozone is a lung irritant and most ionic purifiers create it.

Not sure what you need? We'll come out, assess your home, and recommend only what actually helps.

Frequently asked

Do air purifiers actually help with allergies?

Yes, when you pick the right one. MERV 13 filters and higher capture the particles that cause most pollen, dust, and dander reactions. HEPA systems capture even more. Ionic and ozone generators do not help and can make asthma worse.

What MERV rating should I use?

MERV 11 is a safe upgrade for most homes. MERV 13 is ideal if your system can handle the pressure drop (most modern systems can). Higher than MERV 13 usually requires a larger filter cabinet. We'll check your airflow before recommending.

Are whole-home HEPA systems worth it?

For households with serious allergies, asthma, multiple pets, or someone immunocompromised, yes. For a typical family without symptoms, probably not. Start with a MERV 13 upgrade first and see if that's enough.

Do UV lights kill viruses?

The benefit for airborne virus inactivation is marginal at best. UV lights mounted at the evaporator coil do real work preventing mold and biofilm, which is why we install them for that purpose, not the virus claims.

Can I just buy a plug-in air purifier instead?

Sometimes that's the right call. A good HEPA plug-in purifier in a bedroom works well and is cheap. Whole-home systems make sense when the whole house needs help, not just one room.
