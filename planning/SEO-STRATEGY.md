# SEO Strategy — Maverick & Miller HVAC

Synthesis of all Phase 0 research. Read this first, then the supporting docs.

## ⚠️ Critical finding: brand collision

**M&M Heating & Cooling (mandmheating.com) has owned the Toledo HVAC market since 1980.** They're Toledo's #1 Trane dealer, 45+ years, family-owned, ~4.5 Google reviews. Our client "Maverick & Miller HVAC" abbreviates to M&M.

**Impact:**
- Branded search ("M&M HVAC Toledo") will pull the established company
- Local pack queries ("hvac toledo") will compete directly with a much stronger brand
- AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity) asked about "M&M Toledo HVAC" will cite the established one
- Maverick's own GBP may get suppressed under "similar businesses" treatment

**Recommendation for Janon:**
Talk to Shane + Kindle about this. Options:
1. **Stay the course** — accept collision, emphasize "Maverick & Miller" full name in all copy, never abbreviate to M&M, lean hard on founder story + family differentiator
2. **Rebrand** — harder conversation but cleaner long-term ("Maverick Heating & Cooling", "Miller HVAC", etc.)
3. **Hybrid** — Keep legal name, market as "Maverick" (drop "& Miller" from visible branding)

**What this build assumes: Option 1** (stay the course). Every page uses full "Maverick & Miller HVAC." No M&M abbreviation anywhere in copy or filenames. Differentiator = founders' story + 2024 founding year + A+ BBB (fastest BBB A+ in recent memory).

If Janon gets different direction from client, we revisit the content before launch.

## Market read

- **Saturation**: Toledo HVAC is crowded (M&M, Fry, Bluflame, Glass City, Pugh, Commodore, Maumee Valley, Action). Every core keyword has a local pack + 5+ entrenched service-page competitors.
- **SEO leader**: Fry Heating (80+ pages, 18 city pages, FAQ hub, 1,000+ reviews). Hard to beat, but they feel corporate, not local.
- **Weakest link**: 3 of top 5 competitors have NO schema markup, NO FAQ pages, NO city pages. Technical SEO + programmatic city coverage = our entry point.
- **Clean openings**: Whitehouse and Waterville city queries have thin SERPs — realistic short-term wins. Perrysburg + Sylvania + Bowling Green are crowded but winnable with better local detail.

## Strategic positioning

### What we lead with
1. **Founders' story** — Shane Miller + Kindle VanWormer, "two guys, three decades" (already on site). Real people beats "family-owned since 1965" when competitors all use that line.
2. **A+ BBB in year one** — legitimate differentiator. Most new HVAC companies don't have this.
3. **Founded 2024** — frame as "new generation of service" not "unproven newcomer"
4. **Transparency** — specific services, specific coverage, honest pricing ranges (not hidden quotes)

### What we DON'T do
- Don't claim things we can't back (no "#1 in Toledo", no "24/7" unless client confirms)
- Don't compete on "decades of experience" — we lose that fight
- Don't abbreviate to M&M anywhere

## The 18-page plan (proof of local)

Matches SERP page-type expectations (service pages for service queries, city pages for geo queries). Builds a square internal linking matrix (7 services × 5 cities = 35 link contexts) that Google reads as comprehensive coverage.

Per KEYWORD-MAP.md, page priorities:

**High-priority (build first):**
1. Home (/) — brand + local positioning
2. /contact — converts branded searchers
3. /hvac-whitehouse-oh — low difficulty, quick win
4. /hvac-waterville-oh — low difficulty, quick win
5. /air-filtration — low-medium difficulty
6. /hvac-maintenance — medium difficulty

**Medium-priority:**
7-11. /services overview, /service-area overview, /about, /reviews
12-14. /ac-repair, /furnace-repair — high difficulty but required core pages
15. /water-heater-installation

**Lower-priority / harder SERPs:**
16. /ductless-mini-splits
17. /hvac-perrysburg-oh, /hvac-sylvania-oh, /hvac-bowling-green-oh — crowded SERPs
18. /commercial-hvac — entrenched B2B competitors, hardest

All 18 ship in this build. Order above is "stay focused on quality for the ones that matter most."

## Content pillars

Each service page gets:
1. Clear service definition (no fluff)
2. Signs you need this service (problem-recognition content)
3. What our process looks like (transparent, specific)
4. Pricing guidance (honest range, not "call for quote" only)
5. Service area grid (links to 5 city pages)
6. FAQ (3-5 Qs, FAQPage schema) — targets People Also Ask + AI answer engines
7. CTA: phone + form placeholder

Each city page gets:
1. City-specific intro (2-3 named neighborhoods/subdivisions)
2. Housing stock context (unique per city)
3. Drive time from Toledo 43617 (unique per city)
4. Services grid (7 service links, contextualized)
5. Local FAQ (city-specific Qs)
6. CTA

## E-E-A-T plan (matters for AI citation readiness)

- **Experience**: Founder bios on /about with specific background (years in trade, prior employers if shareable, certifications)
- **Expertise**: OH HVAC license number visible on every page footer, NATE / EPA 608 / ACCA certifications if applicable
- **Authoritativeness**: BBB A+ badge, Trane/Carrier/Rheem dealer badges if applicable, industry memberships
- **Trustworthiness**: Consistent NAP, real reviews (v2 — placeholders for now), clear warranties/guarantees (once client confirms)

## AI SEO / GEO angle

From KEYWORD-MAP.md findings: Toledo HVAC competitors write in marketing voice ("leveraging years of experience to deliver unmatched comfort"). ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini cite **specific, factual, plain-prose** content. This is our opening.

Answer these questions directly, in plain prose, on the right pages:
- "Who is Maverick & Miller HVAC?" → /about
- "How much does furnace repair cost in Toledo?" → /furnace-repair (honest range)
- "Do I need a permit to install a water heater in Toledo?" → /water-heater-installation
- "What size AC do I need for a 1,800 sq ft home?" → /ac-repair
- "Does Maverick & Miller serve Perrysburg?" → /hvac-perrysburg-oh

No marketing fluff in these answer blocks. No "here at Maverick & Miller, we pride ourselves..." Just answers.

## Success targets (12 months post-launch)

- GSC impressions on "hvac toledo" variants: visible within 8 weeks
- First page (top 10) for ≥1 city query within 12 weeks (Whitehouse or Waterville likely first)
- Top 20 local pack for "hvac toledo" within 6 months
- ≥25 referring domains (per BACKLINK-STRATEGY.md)
- ≥5 Google reviews (so AggregateRating schema unlocks)

## What kills this plan

- Pushing thin city pages (Google penalty risk, per PROGRAMMATIC-PLAN.md quality gates)
- Fabricating reviews, warranties, certifications (per Janon's no-fabricated-claims rule)
- Abbreviating to M&M anywhere
- Copying competitor copy
- Launching without GSC/BBB/GBP set up
- Falling into AI-writing voice (humanizer pass catches this)
