Hot water, installed right, permit pulled.

Tank or tankless, gas or electric. Full replacement typically same-day once the unit's on site. We handle the permit and the inspection.

Signs it's time to replace

Water heaters don't usually give a lot of warning. A few things to watch:

  • Tank is 10+ years old (gas) or 12+ (electric)
  • Rusty or discolored hot water
  • Water pooling around the base of the tank
  • Running out of hot water faster than it used to
  • Popping or rumbling sounds during heating cycles (sediment buildup)
  • Higher gas or electric bills with no other change

A leaking tank is not repairable. Once the inner lining fails, replacement is the only option.

Tank versus tankless

Short version: tank is cheaper upfront and simpler. Tankless costs more to install but lasts roughly twice as long and gives endless hot water.

Tank makes sense for most Toledo homes. Reliable, straightforward, 8 to 12 year lifespan. Typical install cost is lower.

Tankless makes sense if you have a large family, run out of hot water regularly, have the gas line capacity (most gas tankless needs a 3/4" line), and plan to stay in the house 10+ years.

Tank leaking? Turn the shut-off valve and call us. Same-day replacement often available.

Frequently asked

Tankless versus tank in Ohio's climate?

Tank works fine in Ohio winters. Tankless also works, but the incoming cold-ground water is colder in Ohio than in warmer states, so you need to size up. We spec tankless units conservatively to handle February groundwater temperatures.

How long does a water heater install take?

Direct swap on a gas tank is usually 2 to 4 hours. Electric tank is similar. Tankless is typically 4 to 8 hours, sometimes a full day if venting or gas line changes are needed.

What size water heater do I need?

Rough rule: 40 gallons for 1 to 2 people, 50 gallons for 3 to 4, 75+ for 5 or more. Tankless is sized by flow rate (gallons per minute), not gallons. We'll size yours based on bathroom count and usage habits.

Do I need a permit for water heater replacement in Toledo?

Yes. Toledo requires a mechanical permit for any water heater install. We pull it and handle the inspection. Permit fee is rolled into our quote.

Can you do same-day replacement?

Usually yes if your existing unit is a standard size and we have stock. Call first thing in the morning. For tankless or unusual tank sizes, we typically schedule within a few days.
