Maintenance & Tune-Ups

Catches the $200 problem before it becomes the $6,000 one.

Spring AC tune-ups in April or May. Fall furnace tune-ups in September or October. Two visits a year, problems caught early, equipment lasts longer.

Why maintenance pays for itself

The straight answer: manufacturers void warranties on neglected equipment. Insurance companies push back on claims when maintenance records don't exist. Energy bills creep up quietly as systems lose efficiency. And the January furnace failure you could have seen coming in October is a $1,500 emergency instead of a $200 tune-up.

A furnace that gets an annual tune-up lasts roughly 20 to 25% longer than one that doesn't. Same with AC units.

What's included in an AC tune-up

  • Refrigerant pressure check (top off up to 1 lb if needed)
  • Coil inspection and cleaning (indoor and outdoor)
  • Capacitor test
  • Contactor inspection
  • Blower wheel check
  • Drain line flush
  • Thermostat calibration
  • Filter check and replacement if you have one on hand
  • Full system operation test
  • Written report of anything we noticed

What's included in a furnace tune-up

  • Burner inspection and cleaning
  • Heat exchanger visual inspection (cracked heat exchangers are a CO risk, this catches them)
  • Flame sensor cleaning
  • Igniter test
  • Inducer motor check
  • Blower motor check and amp draw test
  • Gas pressure verification
  • CO test at registers (safety-critical)
  • Filter check
  • Thermostat calibration
  • Full system operation test

Frequently asked

How often should HVAC be serviced?

Twice a year. Spring AC tune-up in April or May before the first heat wave. Fall furnace tune-up in September or October before first frost. That's it. Quarterly service plans are a marketing gimmick.

What's included in a tune-up?

Full system inspection and cleaning, refrigerant check (AC), combustion check and CO test (furnace), filter check, capacitor and contactor tests, written report. Takes 30 to 60 minutes per unit.

Does maintenance extend equipment life?

Yes. Manufacturer data and real-world experience both show 15 to 25% longer equipment life with annual maintenance versus no maintenance. Also fewer emergency breakdowns.

Is a service plan worth it?

If you have any repair in a given year, yes. 15% off one $600 repair is $90, which means the plan effectively costs $160 for two tune-ups, cheaper than buying them one-off. If nothing breaks, it's break-even or slightly over.

Do tune-ups actually find problems?

Often yes. Cracked heat exchangers, leaking capacitors, worn blower belts, failing igniters, weak capacitors, dirty flame sensors. These are all small problems caught early that would otherwise become winter emergencies.
