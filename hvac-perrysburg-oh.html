Working in Perrysburg

Perrysburg's housing stock splits roughly into two eras. The core historic district has homes built in the 1920s through 1940s, often with original ductwork sized for much smaller loads, plaster walls that don't love being opened up for modern duct runs, and occasionally steam or hot-water boiler systems that need specialized replacement.

Then there's the Levis Commons, Three Meadows, and Eckel Junction corridors, built from the late 1990s onward, with modern forced-air systems, proper duct sizing, and standard equipment that's straightforward to service or replace.

We do a lot of mini-split retrofits in the historic district because central air's a pain to add when you have plaster walls and old ductwork that can't keep up. Newer builds get standard AC or furnace replacements, usually as like-for-like swaps.

Response time to Perrysburg from our Toledo shop is typically 15 to 20 minutes depending on whether we're crossing the Maumee on the Anthony Wayne or the 75 bridge.
